# Design Guidelines

## Core concept
Korean in sensibility, but not decorative or traditionalist. The site must feel contemporary, refined, simple, and highly legible.

## Visual language
- Dark charcoal and black foundation
- Warm ivory primary text
- Restrained bronze or muted gold accents
- Watercolor and ink-wash atmosphere
- Thin lines inspired by the haegeum bow and horsehair
- Spacious, asymmetric editorial composition
- Clear information hierarchy

## Avoid
- Dancheong, clouds, lotus flowers, and repeated traditional motifs
- Hanbok-led branding
- Excessive gold borders or ornamental frames
- Oversized typography that overwhelms the artist image
- Dense card grids and dashboard-like layouts
- Small, low-contrast body text
- Heavy animation and unnecessary 3D effects

## Hero direction
- Use the approved watercolor artwork as the main visual reference
- `CHO YOUN KYOUNG` appears discreetly in the header
- The recital title is the primary message, but must remain proportionate
- Menu is placed horizontally at the top on desktop
- Mobile uses the artist name and a hamburger button
- Date, venue, and `VIEW PERFORMANCE →` remain immediately visible

## Typography
- Korean display headings: refined contemporary Myeongjo style
- English display headings: restrained serif
- Navigation, metadata, and body: highly legible sans serif
- Avoid extremely large text; preserve negative space and elegance

## Interaction
- Simple fade and mask reveals
- Subtle parallax only where it supports the watercolor image
- No auto-rotating hero carousel in the initial release
- One featured hero item at a time
- Performance and album archives expand below or through separate pages

## Accessibility and responsive rules
- Mobile-first layout
- Strong text/background contrast
- Comfortable body size and line height
- Keyboard-accessible navigation and modal leaflet preview
- Respect reduced-motion preferences
